Dave the Perpetual Student

I'm looking to transition from being a stay-at-home dad to being some sort of technical professional. I worked to stay up to date by taking one or two enginnering classes at a time.

I have an A.A.S. in electronics/robotics and a general associate of science degree from the local community college. I've worked as an electronics assembler, machine operator, electronics technician, test-engineering technician, technical trainer, math tutor, and electrical engineering tutor. I like technical work, but my empathic nature has steered me towards tutoring and training as well.

In the past few years, I've switched over to studying the software side of things and steered my academic path towards studying computer, rather than electrical engineering. I was finding more and more that building real-life gizmos requires knowledge of code. After taking a few classes in computer science (C, C++, Java, Python, Discrete Math, Algorithms), it seemed that the young engineering students at university had picked up some web development skills. This didn't seem like a problem until I took an operating systems class and it was assumed to be pre-requisite knowledge. Being a bit older, I needed to dedicate some time to learn it. So for now, I am studying web development and data science. Making robots work is the thrill I would like to get back to.
